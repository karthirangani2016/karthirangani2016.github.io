What is the 'Edge of the Circle' strategy in BR?
The Edge strategy involves staying near the blue zone boundary and moving in with it. This minimizes the chances of being attacked from behind, though it requires constant awareness of the timer and potential gatekeepers.
How do you handle a 'Third-Party' situation?
A third-party situation occurs when two teams are fighting and a third team intervenes. Tactically, you should wait for one team to be eliminated or weakened before engaging, ensuring you don't become the target of both groups.
What is the importance of 'High Ground' in PUBG and BGMI?
High ground provides superior visibility and makes it harder for enemies to take cover. It also allows for easier headshots and gives you the initiative to retreat behind the ridge if you take too much damage.
How should you manage your inventory in a final circle?
In the final circle, prioritize throwables like Smoke Grenades and Frag Grenades over excessive ammo. Smokes are essential for rotating across open fields, while Frags can flush out enemies from behind cover without exposing yourself.
What is 'Gatekeeping' in Battle Royale?
Gatekeeping is the tactic of positioning yourself inside the safe zone to intercept players who are running in from the blue zone. It is a high-reward strategy as the incoming players are often distracted and low on health.
When should you use a Vehicle in the late game?
In the late game, vehicles should be used primarily as mobile cover or for extremely fast rotations to center-zone compounds. Be aware that vehicles draw significant attention and can be easily destroyed by concentrated fire.
What is 'Snaking' and how do you counter it?
Snaking is the act of prone-crawling in tall grass to remain hidden. To counter it, use high ground to look down into the grass, or use vehicles to flush out snakes. Frag grenades and Molotovs are also highly effective.
What is the 'Center of the Circle' strategy?
The Center strategy involves rushing to the middle of the safe zone early to secure a strong compound. This reduces the need for mid-game rotations but makes you a target for multiple teams looking for a place to stay.
How do you win a 1v4 situation?
To win a 1v4, you must force four 1v1 engagements. Use cover to isolate one enemy at a time, change your position constantly after every knock, and use throwables to prevent them from reviving their teammates.
What is 'Bridge Camping'?
Bridge camping involves blocking a bridge to eliminate players who must cross it to reach the safe zone. It is a classic ambush tactic that relies on the lack of alternative routes across water.
