What is 'Social Contract' theory?
Social contract theory is the view that persons' moral and/or political obligations are dependent upon a contract or agreement among them to form the society in which they live.
What is 'Macroeconomics'?
Macroeconomics is a branch of economics dealing with the performance, structure, behavior, and decision-making of an economy as a whole, rather than individual markets.
What is the 'Periodic Table'?
The periodic table is a tabular display of the chemical elements, organized by atomic number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties.
What is 'DNA Replication'?
DNA replication is the biological process of producing two identical replicas of DNA from one original DNA molecule. It is the basis for biological inheritance.
What is 'Thermodynamics'?
Thermodynamics is the branch of physics that has to do with heat and temperature and their relation to energy and work. It is governed by four laws.
What is 'Occam's Razor'?
Occam's Razor is the problem-solving principle that, when presented with competing hypotheses, one should select the one that makes the fewest assumptions.
What is 'Deep Learning'?
Deep learning is part of a broader family of machine learning methods based on artificial neural networks with representation learning. It can be supervised, semi-supervised or unsupervised.
What is 'Nanotechnology'?
Nanotechnology is the manipulation of matter on an atomic, molecular, and supramolecular scale. It has applications in medicine, electronics, and materials science.
What is 'Cognitive Dissonance'?
Cognitive dissonance is the mental discomfort experienced by a person who holds two or more contradictory beliefs, ideas, or values.
What is 'Zero-Sum Game'?
In game theory and economic theory, a zero-sum game is a mathematical representation of a situation in which each participant's gain or loss of utility is exactly balanced by the losses or gains of the utility of the other participants.
