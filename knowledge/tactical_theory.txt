What is 'The Art of War' by Sun Tzu?
'The Art of War' is an ancient Chinese military treatise attributed to Sun Tzu. it focuses on strategy, tactics, and the importance of psychological warfare and deception in achieving victory.
What is 'Asymmetric Warfare'?
Asymmetric warfare is a type of conflict between two groups whose relative military power, strategy, or tactics differ significantly. It often involves a smaller, insurgent force using guerrilla tactics against a much larger, conventional military.
What is a 'Force Multiplier'?
A force multiplier is a factor or a combination of factors that gives a personnel or a weapon system the ability to accomplish greater feats than without it. Examples include technology, morale, or superior positioning.
What is the 'OODA Loop'?
The OODA Loop (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act) is a decision-making cycle developed by military strategist John Boyd. It emphasizes the importance of cycling through these stages faster than your opponent to seize the initiative.
What is 'Cyber Kinetic' warfare?
Cyber Kinetic warfare refers to cyber attacks that result in physical damage or loss of life, such as taking down a power grid or interfering with a dam's control system.
What is 'Defense in Depth'?
Defense in Depth is a security strategy that uses multiple layers of defensive controls throughout an information technology system. If one layer fails, others are in place to stop the threat.
What is 'Guerrilla Warfare'?
Guerrilla warfare is a form of irregular warfare in which small groups of combatants, such as paramilitary personnel, armed civilians, or irregulars, use military tactics including ambushes, sabotage, raids, petty warfare, hit-and-run tactics, and mobility to fight a larger and less-mobile traditional military.
What is 'Information Superiority'?
Information superiority is the operational advantage derived from the ability to collect, process, and disseminate an uninterrupted flow of information while exploiting or denying an adversary's ability to do the same.
